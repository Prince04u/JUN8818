Responsible Gaming

Practical frameworks, platform tools and confidential support to keep entertainment entertainment.

Gaming is designed to be entertainment, and entertainment has a budget. Responsible play starts with three small decisions made before a session begins: how much time you'll spend, how much money you're prepared to lose, and what cue will tell you to stop.

Safe Gaming Practices

  • Decide your time and money limits before you open the app.
  • Use deposit, loss and session limits — every regulated platform offers them.
  • Never chase losses; chasing is the strongest predictor of harmful play.
  • Keep gambling and credit separate. Funded play only.
  • Take a 24-hour break after any session that felt out of control.

Time Management

Set a hard session timer on your phone, not inside the casino client. Most platforms also offer "reality check" pop-ups every 30 or 60 minutes — turn them on. Long, uninterrupted sessions impair judgment, the same way long driving stretches do.

Budget Control

Use a dedicated payment method for entertainment spend. E-wallets that you top up manually are ideal because they create a natural circuit-breaker. If you ever consider increasing a deposit limit, sleep on the decision; the cool-off is there for a reason.

Gaming Awareness

Be honest with yourself about why you're logging in. Boredom, loneliness, stress and chasing losses are warning signs. Gaming should sit alongside other forms of entertainment — not replace them.

Support Resources

If gaming stops being fun

Reach out — confidentially and free.

  • GamCare (UK) — 24/7 helpline and live chat
  • BeGambleAware — information, self-assessment and counselling
  • National Council on Problem Gambling (US) — call/text 1-800-GAMBLER
  • Gambling Therapy — global online support in multiple languages
How do I know if I should take a break?

If gaming sessions are pushing past your planned time, eating into your budget or affecting your mood the next day, that's a signal to step back. Most platforms now offer one-click cool-off periods of 24 hours to several weeks.

What are deposit limits?

Deposit limits are voluntary caps you set on how much you can transfer into your account in a given day, week or month. They typically take effect immediately when reduced, and have a delay when raised.

Where can I get confidential support?

Organizations like GamCare, BeGambleAware and the National Council on Problem Gambling offer free, confidential helplines and live chat 24/7 in many regions.
